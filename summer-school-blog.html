Starting Summer School
& How I'm Feeling About It

☀️ Summer 2025
📖 Personal
💬 My Thoughts
Hey Everyone! 👋

Okay so I know summer is supposed to be all about sleeping in, swimming, and doing absolutely nothing for like three months. And honestly? That sounds AMAZING. But this year is a little different for me — I'm starting summer school.


I'm not gonna lie, when I first found out I had to go, I was SO upset. Like, all my friends are at the pool and I have to get up early and go to class? That felt really unfair. But I decided to write about it because writing helps me figure out how I feel about stuff.


So here's my honest, real, no-filter thoughts on summer school so far. 🙃

Real talk: I cried the night before the first day. 😭 But I'm still here and I survived, so that's something!
How I Felt on Day One 😬

Walking in on the first morning was SO awkward. I didn't know who was going to be in my class and I was worried I'd be the only person from my school. Spoiler: I wasn't, but it still felt weird.


Here's the honest list of feelings I had:


  • 😒Kind of annoyed that summer was "ruined"
  • 😰Nervous about not knowing anyone
  • 😳Embarrassed because I thought it meant I was "dumb"
  • 😤A tiny bit mad (okay, a LOT mad)
  • 😶Trying to keep it together and not show any of that
The Thing About Summer School 💭

Here's what I've been thinking about. Summer school doesn't mean you're dumb. I know that in my head, but it's harder to believe it in your heart sometimes, you know?


My mom told me that summer school means someone cares enough to make sure I don't fall behind. That it's actually a GOOD thing — like a second chance or extra help, not a punishment. I'm still working on fully believing that, but I think she has a point.


Some of the smartest people I know needed extra help with stuff. That doesn't make them any less smart. It just means they needed a different way to learn something. And that's… actually okay?

Something my mom said that I keep thinking about: "The kids who ask for help are the bravest ones." 💙
Okay But Some Things Are Actually Good 😅

I hate to admit this but… it's not as bad as I thought it would be. Here's what I've noticed:


  • ✅The classes are smaller so the teacher actually knows your name
  • ✅We get more time to actually understand stuff instead of rushing
  • ✅I met a girl named Maya who is really funny and we eat lunch together now
  • ✅It ends early enough that I can still do stuff in the afternoon
  • ✅I feel like I'm actually starting to understand the stuff I was confused about

That last one is the biggest one. There was this topic in math that I always felt lost on and acted like I understood it when I didn't. But now I actually GET it. And that feeling is kind of amazing.

💡 Understanding something you used to be confused about is one of the best feelings EVER. Like your brain just leveled up! 🎮
What I'm Learning About Myself 🌱

This is the part I didn't expect at ALL. I thought summer school would just be boring review stuff. But I'm learning things about myself too.


I'm learning that I give up too fast when something is hard. Like, the second I feel confused I kind of check out and hope no one notices. But in a smaller class you can't really hide, so I've had to actually ask questions. And you know what? Nobody laughed. The teacher just… answered. It was fine.


I'm also learning that doing something hard, even when you don't want to, builds something inside you. I don't know the exact word for it but it feels like… strength? Confidence? Something good.

Note to my future self reading this: You did the hard thing. That matters. 🌟
My Goals for the Rest of Summer School 🎯
  • 📚Actually pay attention every single day, even when I'm tired
  • 🙋🏽‍♀️Raise my hand more, even when I'm not 100% sure
  • 😴Go to bed at a decent time so I'm not falling asleep in class
  • 📝Keep writing in this blog about how things are going
  • 🤝Be kind to the other kids — we're all in this together
  • 🏆Finish strong and walk out of there PROUD
My Final Thoughts (For Now) 💛

If you're reading this and you're also going to summer school, or you had to do something this summer that you didn't want to do — I see you. It's okay to be upset about it at first. Those feelings are real.


But I also want to say: you might surprise yourself. I know I'm surprising myself. Every morning I wake up and I go, and every day I come home knowing a little more than I did before. That has to count for something.


Summer school isn't the end of summer. It's just a different kind of summer. And maybe, just maybe, I'll look back on this one as the summer I got stronger. 💪🏽


Thanks for reading my blog. I'll keep writing. ✏️


— Me 🌻
